# Symbol vocabulary

The 16 slugs used for atlas classification and Symbol Card resolution in the Rêveries app. Each slug maps a class of dream image to a Jungian register: not a fixed meaning, but a field of amplification that the image is likely to be working within.

A slug is not an interpretation. When a dream is classified as `water`, it means water of some kind is the dominant image and the associated Jungian register is relevant background. What the water means for this dreamer, in this dream, at this moment, is not determined by the slug. That is Marie-Louise's work, and it cannot be automated.

## The vocabulary

**water**

The unconscious as it presents itself most directly: the maternal waters, the depths beneath the surface of waking life, the medium through which transformation occurs. Water dreams cluster around threshold moments, before a significant change, during a period of dissolution, at the edge of something not yet named. In the corpus, water appears more frequently than any other element, which is itself worth sitting with.

**shadow**

The rejected or unacknowledged dimension of the personality, appearing in dreams as a figure of the same gender as the dreamer who carries qualities the dreamer has not yet owned. The shadow is unintegrated, not evil. Its appearance in a dream is usually a signal that something is asking to be recognised rather than continued to be refused.

**fire**

Transformation, libido in its original Jungian sense, the energy that both creates and destroys. Fire dreams often accompany periods of significant psychological movement, not always comfortable, not always chosen. The alchemical register is relevant here: fire as the agent of transmutation rather than simply destruction.

**descent**

The katabasis, the underworld journey, the movement into what lies beneath. Descent dreams include falling, going underground, entering basements or caves, moving toward darkness. The Jungian tradition reads descent not as pathology but as initiation: the necessary movement downward before any genuine ascent becomes possible.

**ascent**

The complementary movement upward, toward light, toward transcendence. Ascent dreams can carry genuine spiritual content or the inflation of an ego that has not yet done its descent work. The distinction matters and is one of the harder calls in the classification.

**animal**

The instinctual dimension of the psyche, the natural self that precedes and underlies the ego's constructions. Animals in dreams often carry a quality of wisdom or of energy that the dreamer's conscious life has lost contact with. The specific animal matters enormously: this slug is a starting point, not a conclusion.

**house-known**

The self as dwelling, the familiar psyche, the interior landscape the dreamer has already mapped. Known house dreams often surface at moments when that interior landscape is being questioned or reorganised.

**house-unknown**

The unexplored dimensions of the self, the rooms not yet entered, the structure of the psyche as it extends beyond what the ego currently knows. Unknown house dreams frequently accompany significant developmental periods.

**child**

The original self, the self before the ego's adaptations, the divine child archetype that carries both vulnerability and enormous potential. Child dreams often appear when something early and uncorrupted is trying to resurface, or when the dreamer is being asked to recover a quality of openness that adult life has covered over.

**elder**

The wise old man or wise old woman, the accumulated wisdom of the psyche, the figure who knows more than the ego currently does and offers it without coercion. Elder dreams often mark a turning point: something is being handed forward.

**mother**

The Great Mother archetype in its full ambivalence: nourishing and devouring, protective and suffocating, the origin and the obstacle. Mother dreams rarely land cleanly. The specific quality of the mother figure in the dream is more important than the fact of her presence.

**father**

Authority, law, the structuring principle, the senex. Father dreams carry similar ambivalence to mother dreams: the father who enables and the father who constrains are often the same figure at different moments.

**journey**

Individuation as movement, the quest, the road as the image of a life being lived toward something. Journey dreams often carry a sense of direction even when the destination is unclear, which is itself significant.

**door**

The threshold, the moment of transition, the liminal point between one state and another. Door dreams cluster around decisions, around passages that cannot be undone, around the moment before entering something new.

**eye**

Consciousness, the witness, the Self in its observing aspect. Eye dreams often carry a numinous quality: the sense of being seen by something larger than the dreamer's ordinary awareness.

**fool**

The unresolved state, the image that holds its shape before it yields a name. When a dream does not resolve clearly to any other slug, it lands here. The fool is the honest acknowledgment that not every dream yields to this vocabulary, and that some images need to be sat with before they reveal their register.

## Proposing a new symbol

If you encounter a class of dream that consistently fails to map to any of these slugs, the contributing guide explains how to bring that observation forward. The vocabulary is not complete. It was built from one corpus and it will have gaps that only become visible through use.
