# A writing guide for Rêveries

*Voice · Vocabulary · Principles*

## What this voice is

Someone who has thought about this for a long time, who does not need to perform seriousness because the seriousness is already present in the material, and who trusts the reader to arrive at the same gravity without being pointed toward it. The writing feels like it was written for someone who already cares, not for someone who needs to be convinced to care.

It admits its own situation honestly. It thinks aloud. It ends with genuine questions, not rhetorical ones. It is warm because it is actually interested, not because warmth has been decided upon as a strategy.

## AI vocabulary — words to avoid

These words are not wrong. They are precise. That is the problem — they signal a mind optimising for clarity rather than a person thinking through something.

| Word or phrase | Why it sounds like AI | What to use instead |
|---|---|---|
| Furthermore | Signals list-building. No human adds to a thought this way in natural speech. | and beyond that / there is also / what makes this stranger still |
| Additionally | Pure enumeration. Closes the previous thought before opening the next. | on top of that / and then there is / which also means |
| It is worth noting | Tells the reader what to notice rather than just noticing it. | just say the thing |
| It is important to | Announces importance rather than demonstrating it. | drop it — the sentence that follows should earn its own importance |
| In this context | Filler. The context is usually already established. | here / in practice / for a dream like this |
| This represents | Interposes the writer between the thing and the reader. | this is / what this does |
| Ultimately | Signals a conclusion is coming. The sentence that follows usually doesn't need the warning. | in the end / at bottom / or just drop it |
| Leveraging | Business register. Treats everything as a resource to be deployed. | using / drawing on / working with |
| Robust | Technical approval word. | solid / reliable / it holds / it actually works |
| Seamlessly | Marketing register. Nothing is seamless. | without friction / quietly / in practice it just works |
| Delve into | One of the most recognisable AI phrases. | go into / look at / get into |
| Nuanced | Claims complexity without demonstrating it. | show the complexity instead of naming it |
| Comprehensive | Self-approving. | complete / covers all of it / as far as I can tell |
| Facilitate | Bureaucratic. Distances the writer from the action. | help / make possible / allow / open up |
| In order to | Usually "to" does the same work. | to |
| It is not X, it is Y | Pre-empts misreading. Often means Y hasn't been thought through enough to stand alone. | just say Y / if the contrast is necessary, earn it first |
| Groundbreaking | Empty superlative. | new / the first time / something that hasn't been done before |
| Moving forward | Corporate transition phrase. | from here / next / what happens now |

## French-inflected English

The founder's first language is French. Certain French-origin words carry more precision and texture in this register than their Anglo-Saxon equivalents. Where culturally recognised and not forced, prefer these:

| Word | When to use it |
|---|---|
| *milieu* | the surrounding atmosphere or collective context |
| *rapport* | the quality of a connection, not just its existence |
| *contour* | the shape of something as it emerges |
| *residue* | what remains after something has passed through, still carrying the quality of what left it |
| *register* | the mode or pitch at which something addresses you |
| *provenance* | where something comes from and what that origin means |
| *bricolage* | something built from what was available rather than what was planned |
| *élan* | forward movement that has spirit and conviction in it |
| *frisson* | a charge of recognition that is physical as well as intellectual |
| *dépaysement* | the productive disorientation of being somewhere that reframes you |
| *je ne sais quoi* | when the indefinability is precisely the point |

The principle: these words earn their place when they are the most precise word available, not because they add texture. One or two per piece at most. When in doubt, the simpler word is better.

## Principles

### On admitting the obvious

The most disarming thing a piece of writing can do is name its own situation honestly before the reader names it. If you are building something and writing about it, say so. The admission does not undermine credibility — it establishes it, because the reader stops waiting for the thing to be revealed and can simply be present with what is actually being said.

### On thinking aloud

Parenthetical asides, small qualifications, the occasional sentence that changes direction mid-thought — these are not weaknesses to be edited out. They are the texture of a mind that is genuinely present in the writing rather than managing it from a distance.

### On short declarative sentences

A sequence of short declarative sentences produces a rhythm that feels authoritative but reads as performance. When sentences are short because the thought is complete, that is good. When they are short because the writer stopped before the thought was finished, the reader feels it as abruptness dressed as confidence. Test: read the sequence aloud. If the pauses feel like emphasis, they are earned. If they feel like someone waiting to be agreed with, the sentences want joining.

### On earning your abstractions

Abstract words earn their place by arriving after their ground has been prepared. Placed before the concrete, abstraction reads as decoration.

### On warmth

Warmth in writing is not a register or a tone. It is a consequence of the writer being genuinely interested in the person they are writing for. It cannot be performed.

### On the open question

Ending with a genuine question — not a rhetorical one, not one that points back at the argument, but one that actually wants an answer — is one of the most honest things writing can do. It signals that the writer has not closed the subject.
